Origin Story

From Tesla dreams to Python plot twist

I started studying electronic engineering with dreams of working at Tesla. Small problem: circuits and I were as compatible as vim and emacs. But when I discovered I was way better with computers, I realized my destiny was bits, not volts. Plot twist: now I program AI that could drive those Teslas.

Experience

The art of productive burnout

Post-bachelor's, total burnout. Solution? Go work at Luxottica (because nothing says "rest" like managing a team of 5 suppliers and implementing AGVs in production). There I discovered I could make Naive Bayes coexist with logistics and that predicting production problems was more fun than solving them. Bonus: I learned that managing people is like debugging, but with more coffee and less Stack Overflow.

The comeback: From zero to ML hero

Recovered from burnout, I returned to university for my master's in Data Science. I got so passionate about deep learning that I created my own PyTorch library (stockpy) - because apparently I didn't have enough problems in my life. From there, the addiction to side projects became chronic.
